01 — Foundation

What we stand for

Tamayaz exists because the region's most ambitious professionals deserve more than generic career advice translated from English. Our brand reflects that seriousness of purpose — calm, expert, and rooted in the region we serve.

Essence

The quiet expert in your corner — who knows exactly how hiring works here, what recruiters actually look for, and how to position you so you stand out for the right reasons.

02 — Logo

The mark

Our logo is the primary signature of Tamayaz. It carries the weight of the brand in a single mark, designed to feel confident, modern, and unmistakably rooted in Arabic identity.

Logo on backgrounds

The logo must always have sufficient contrast with its background. Use these four approved treatments:

On Navy — Primary dark use
On Ivory — Primary light use
On White — Digital neutral
On Gold — Accent moments only

Clear space & minimum size

Always give the logo room to breathe. The minimum clear space on all sides equals the height of the gold accent in the mark. Minimum digital size: 32px height. Minimum print size: 12mm height.

Do

  • Use the logo in its approved color combinations only
  • Maintain the minimum clear space at all times
  • Use the SVG version for all digital applications
  • Scale the logo proportionally — always lock aspect ratio
  • Place on solid backgrounds with sufficient contrast
  • Use the full lockup (mark + wordmark) as default

Don't

  • Change the colors of the logo — navy and gold are fixed
  • Stretch, skew, rotate, or distort the logo in any way
  • Add effects — no shadows, outlines, gradients, or glows
  • Place on busy photographs or low-contrast backgrounds
  • Recreate the logo in a different font — only use our files
  • Use the logo smaller than 32px or 12mm in height

04 — Typography

Words with weight

Typography is where a brand either earns its premium positioning or loses it. Our system uses two carefully paired fonts — one Arabic, one Latin — chosen to feel like a single voice across both languages.

Display — Editorial moments only

Fraunces · Google Fonts · Free · Used sparingly

Use Fraunces for hero headlines on the marketing site and printed materials only. Never in product UI. It carries editorial weight — use it to set a tone, not as a workhorse.

Line height

Arabic needs more vertical space than Latin. Latin: 1.5 for body, 1.2 for headlines. Arabic: 1.7 for body, 1.3 for headlines. Always test both languages side by side before shipping.

05 — Voice

How we speak

Our voice is the user's trusted advisor — the senior colleague who tells you the truth because they want you to succeed. We are clear, confident, and respectful. We never hype, never patronize, never fake warmth.

Tone

Calm, expert, human

We sound like someone who has been in the GCC hiring market for twenty years — not a product marketer trying to sell you something.

Arabic

فصحى مبسّطة

Simplified Modern Standard Arabic — professional but warm, never stiff. Short sentences. Direct. Respectful.

English

FT reader, not Twitter

Clear, confident, no jargon. No exclamation marks. No emoji in product UI. Precise word choice matters more than clever phrasing.

Perspective

You are the hero

We talk about your career, your CV, your goals. We're the advisor in the background, not the star of the story.

Voice in practice

Yes — like this

"Your CV is ready. We tailored three sections based on the role."

No — not like this

"🎉 Amazing! Your CV is ready to shine! ✨"

Yes — like this

"Two sections need more detail before this CV is ready to send."

No — not like this

"Oops! Looks like we need just a tiny bit more info from you!"

Yes — Arabic

"سيرتك الذاتية جاهزة. عدّلنا ثلاثة أقسام لتناسب هذه الوظيفة."

No — Arabic

"🎊 رائع! سيرتك الذاتية جاهزة وستتألق!"
